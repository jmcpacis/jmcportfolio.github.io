Week 4 of MSHI

In an ideal world, health data can be shared seamlessly whenever, wherever to whomever. Instead, health data are trapped in silos which can compromise care. This week’s driving question gives you a glimpse into health informatics’ enduring challenge, the holy grail of interoperability.

Live Link

Overview

Interoperability is difficult for several reasons, some of which are:

1. Healthcare system complexity with several interoperability layers.
2. Inconsistent data standards across healthcare providers due to a lack of standardization.
3. The use of various systems and technology by healthcare practitioners is met with resistance.
4. Privacy and security issues that make safely exchanging patient data more difficult.

Typically, healthcare groups, government agencies, and other relevant parties work together to set standards. The Philippines has a healthcare regulatory agency or governing body that has a hand in establishing requirements for healthcare data.

Standard-changing procedures typically entail an organized approach that may include the following steps:

1. Recognizing the need for change, which is frequently motivated by developments in technology or modifications to healthcare procedures.
2. Collaboration between relevant stakeholders, including government agencies, healthcare providers, technology vendors, and standards organizations.3. creation of new standards or revisions to current ones using a consensus-based method.
4. The suggested modifications are examined and approved by the appropriate governing bodies or regulatory agencies.
5. Acceptance and implementation of the new guidelines throughout the healthcare industry.
