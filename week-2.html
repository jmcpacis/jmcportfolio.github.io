Week 2 of MSHI

This week’s driving question invites you to examine the Philippines’ national ehealth strategy.

Overview

In Japan, there are four times the number of MRI scanners and six times the number of CT scanners compared with the average numbers they have in Europe. They also have the longest life expectancy in the world, which is MAINLY DUE TO THEIR HIGH-QUALITY HEALTHCARE PROVISION.

This also the case for Taiwan, where they are considered as one of the countries with the best healthcare systems in the world due to their properly managed public health insurance. Now, you might ask, why does the Philippines have poor healthcare system if its neighboring countries are doing well? What can it do to be better?

Right now, the Philippines ranked 120th out of 195 countries, according to a study in 2017. Our Healthcare system can be somehow called as “a work in progress”, and even labeled “fragmented with insufficient government investment, inappropriate incentives from providers, weak social protection and high inequity” by the World health organization. With this evident lack of competency in our healthcare system, there still might be a slim chance of INCREASING our current placement if we would:

First: Enhance leadership and coordination across government agencies responsible for eHealth to ensure a unified approach. This means strengthening the governance structure to oversee eHealth initiatives effectively.

Second: Allocate more resources, both financial and human, to support the implementation of eHealth solutions. Develop a clear and comprehensive national eHealth strategy that outlines specific goals, objectives, and timelines.

Third and lastly: Expand and upgrade the country's ICT infrastructure, including broadband connectivity, to support the growing demand for digital health services. Ensure that remote and underserved areas have access to reliable digital infrastructure.
