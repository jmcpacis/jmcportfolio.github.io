Why I chose MSc Health Informatics as my postgraduate program

As we all know, health informatics focuses on the effective use of information technology, data, and knowledge to support and improve healthcare delivery, patient outcomes, and overall healthcare management.

In the subject of health informatics, information from clinical sciences, fundamental sciences, and biology is retrieved and processed for use in problem-solving and healthcare decision-making. In a much easier understanding, the multidisciplinary area of health informatics employs health information technology to enhance healthcare.

Where you reside will determine your area of specialization. The subject of health informatics aids in gathering health information so that it may be studied and altered to enhance the way that care is currently provided. The use of health informatics helps to spot patterns that may provide light on patient health conditions and care delivery methods. By creating greater knowledge and information from patient data to address medical mistake reduction and care cost reduction, it also aids in enhancing the quality of treatment.

Any health informatician's first priority is to use information technology to offer health care that is higher quality and more widely accessible. By combining their present knowledge with IT knowledge and using it to enhance healthcare and healthcare delivery, professionals from a variety of backgrounds may achieve this aim. The collecting, management, and analysis of patient data inside a hospital or care facility are the main areas of concentration for health informaticians. Another area where a computer science specialist may be useful is in the extraction of large data from the healthcare industry, cleansing the data, and putting it in the right forms for data analytics. In addition, since the field of health informatics plays a huge role in a global manner, a professional from such an integral area is expected to have core values to deal with their jobs accordingly:

1. Effective Use of Biomedical Data and Information: Health informatics workers should be able to use biomedical data, knowledge, and information to conduct research, solve problems, and make decisions.
2. Interdisciplinary Knowledge: They should be able to bridge the gap between data management, information technology, and healthcare.
3. Application of Theories and techniques: Expertise in formulating, putting into practice, and modifying theories, techniques, and procedures for producing, storing, retrieving, managing, and exchanging biological data and information.
4. Technical expertise: The ability to effectively use computer, telecommunication, and information sciences and technologies, with a focus on how they are applied in biomedicine.
5. Understanding of the Human and Social Context: Health informatics experts should use social and behavioral sciences to guide the design, assessment, and implementation of technical solutions, policies, and healthcare systems because they understand that healthcare information ultimately serves people.
